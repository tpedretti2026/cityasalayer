About This Research

Understanding the PhD research trajectory

Research Goal

Support municipalities in making knowledge made public through participatory processes an asset for future urban policies. This research designs an atlas of guidelines, procedures, best practices, and training modules that enhance the ability to collect, maintain visibility, and make accessible the legacy of participatory processes on urban transformation.

Research Question

How can design support municipalities in framing the legacy of participatory processes on urban transformation and make it impactful on different levels: urban, social, cultural, and environmental?

Key Objectives

  1. Collect - Gather knowledge and insights from participatory urban processes
  2. Maintain - Keep participatory knowledge visible and organized over time
  3. Preserve - Create accessible records of the legacy of urban transformation processes
  4. Impact - Ensure participatory knowledge influences future urban policies across multiple dimensions

Research Scope

This research addresses impacts on:

  • Urban Level - City planning and spatial transformation
  • Social Level - Community engagement and inclusion
  • Cultural Level - Preservation of collective knowledge and values
  • Environmental Level - Sustainable and ecological considerations

Key Concepts

  • Participatory processes in urban planning
  • Knowledge management and institutional memory
  • Design as a tool for policy influence
  • Municipal governance and public participation
  • Urban legacy and transformation
